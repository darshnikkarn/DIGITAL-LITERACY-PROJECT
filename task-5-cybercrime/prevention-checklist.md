Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

1.Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on all email, social media, and academic accounts to add a crucial secondary layer of security against unauthorized access.

2.Verify UPI Payment Requests: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. PINs are strictly required only when you are sending funds from your account.

3.Use Official Banking Apps: Only download banking and UPI applications from official sources, and avoid clicking payment links sent via SMS or WhatsApp from unknown numbers.

4.Scrutinize Email Senders: Always check the actual email address of the sender, not just the display name, especially if the email requests urgent action, password resets, or personal information.

5.Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your laptop's operating system, antivirus software, and mobile apps to patch known security vulnerabilities.

6.Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Do not perform financial transactions, access bank accounts, or log into critical student portals while connected to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks in cafes or hostels.

7.Backup Critical Data: Frequently back up your assignments, projects, and personal files to an encrypted cloud service or an external hard drive that is kept disconnected when not in use.

8.Manage Your Digital Footprint: Limit the amount of personal information you share publicly on social media to prevent social engineering attacks and identity theft.
